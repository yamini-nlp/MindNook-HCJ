The Problem

Most journaling apps store your words.
None of them understand you.

Traditional journals are passive notebooks. They can hold a thousand entries and still tell you nothing about how your mood is actually trending, whether today genuinely differs from your baseline, or when a rough patch is starting to look like something more serious.

01
No Personal Reference Point
Your mood gets compared to nobody — or to a generic population average that has nothing to do with your actual history.
02
No Sense of When to Step In
Most AI companions either miss real distress entirely or overreact to a bad joke and an ordinary hard day.
03
No Reasoning You Can Check
You get a response, never the "why." There's no way to see — or challenge — how an AI reached its conclusion.
The Solution

Reflective writing, turned into
measurable self-understanding.

MindNook runs a published five-layer framework on every entry, wraps it in a crisis-aware safety layer, and shows its work at every step — so reflection becomes something you can actually track.

01 — Baseline
Compared Against You, Not a Population
Every entry is scored against your own historical mean, reported as a personal deviation — with a fixed population reference shown alongside it, never in place of it.
02 — Language
Pragmatic and Goal-Aware Analysis
Classifies what you're actually doing when you write — venting, asking for help, stating a fact — and checks it against the goals you've set for yourself.
03 — Trend
Longitudinal Trend Modeling
A recurrent model attends over your recent history and classifies your trajectory as stable, improving, declining, or cyclical.
04 — Safety
Crisis-Aware, Hyperbole-Literate Screening
A dedicated safety layer tells "this ruined my whole week" apart from language that signals genuine risk, before anything reaches the AI.
05 — Intelligence
Nook AI, Reasoning Transparently
Every reply is built from your live framework values and moderated before you see it — and can be expanded into its layer-by-layer reasoning on request.
06 — Control
Calibrated to You, Not a Default
Set your own tolerance for false alarms versus missed signals during onboarding, and let the system adjust — within bounds you control — from your own feedback.

Privacy

Private by design.

MindNook prioritizes psychological safety. Journal entries are analyzed through protected inference pipelines, every inference type is independently controllable, and you keep full ownership over your reflective history.

Protected Inference Pipelines
Every AI call runs server-side through authenticated Supabase Edge Functions. Your entries are never processed by a third-party client SDK directly.
Five Independent Privacy Scopes
Sentiment, pragmatic, temporal, goal-inference, and full-history chat access can each be switched off — and turning one off stops that computation entirely.
Full Export and Deletion Rights
Export a complete JSON copy of your entries and analysis at any time. Deletion is confirmation-gated and cascades across every linked table.
FAQ

Straight answers, before you start.

What makes MindNook different from a regular journaling app?
MindNook scores every entry against your own historical baseline instead of a generic average, then runs four more layers on top — pragmatics, temporal trend, goal alignment, and response strategy — that shape how the AI responds, not just what it says.
Is this based on real research?
Yes. MindNook is the reference implementation of a five-layer framework published as an IEEE TechRxiv preprint, and the app computes all five layers on every entry rather than approximating them.
What happens if I write something concerning?
A client-side, hyperbole-aware safety layer checks for genuine risk language before anything reaches the AI. Figurative language like "this ruined my week" is treated differently from language indicating real risk, and confirmed risk surfaces a crisis-resource banner.
Can I turn off specific types of analysis?
Yes. Sentiment, pragmatic, temporal, goal-inference, and full-history chat access are five independently toggleable privacy scopes, and disabling one stops that computation rather than just hiding its output.
What AI model powers Nook AI?
Nook AI runs on LLaMA 3.3 70B via Groq, called through Supabase Edge Functions, with every reply passed through a moderation check before it reaches you.
Can I delete my data?
Yes, at any time. A confirmation-gated deletion cascades across every user-linked table, and a self-serve export gives you a full JSON copy of your entries and analysis first.
